1. Parties & Scope

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between the entity using HabitAI ("Controller") and the HabitAI team ("Processor"). It governs the processing of personal data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in connection with the use of the HabitAI application and related services.

This DPA applies exclusively when the Controller uses the optional cloud sync feature. When using HabitAI in local-only mode, no personal data is transmitted to or processed by the Processor.

2. Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined herein shall have the meaning given in the General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, "GDPR").

  • "Controller" means the entity that determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data.
  • "Processor" means the entity that processes personal data on behalf of the Controller.
  • "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
  • "Data Subject" means the identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed.
  • "Sub-Processor" means any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
  • "Cloud Sync" means the optional feature that synchronizes habit data across devices via our servers.

3. Details of Processing

Subject matter Processing of habit tracking data, preferences, and authentication information for the purpose of providing cloud sync and optional AI features.
Duration Until the Controller disables cloud sync or deletes their data, or until the agreement is terminated in accordance with the Terms of Service.
Nature and purpose Synchronization of habit data across devices, authentication, and delivery of optional AI assistant features.
Categories of data subjects End users of the HabitAI application who have enabled cloud sync.
Categories of personal data Habit names, schedules, completion logs, streak data, app preferences, and (if cloud sync is enabled) Google account name and email address.

4. Obligations of the Data Processor

The Processor shall:

  • Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required to do so by applicable law.
  • Ensure that persons authorized to process the Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality.
  • Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk.
  • Not engage another Sub-Processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Controller.
  • Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with its obligations under Articles 32 to 36 of the GDPR.
  • At the choice of the Controller, delete or return all Personal Data after the end of the provision of services.
  • Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in Article 28 of the GDPR.

5. Sub-Processors

The Controller authorizes the engagement of the following Sub-Processors:

Sub-Processor Service Location Data Processed
Google Cloud Platform (GCP) Cloud infrastructure and data storage United States (us-central1) Encrypted habit data, account metadata
Google Identity Services Authentication (Google Sign-In) United States Profile name, email address

The Processor shall notify the Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-Processors at least 30 days in advance, giving the Controller an opportunity to object.

6. Data Subject Rights

The Processor shall assist the Controller by implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures to fulfill the Controller's obligation to respond to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under the GDPR, including:

  • Right of access (Article 15 GDPR)
  • Right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR)
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") (Article 17 GDPR)
  • Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR)
  • Right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR)
  • Right to object (Article 21 GDPR)

The Processor shall promptly notify the Controller if it receives a request from a Data Subject and shall not respond to such a request without the Controller's prior authorization, except as required by law.

7. Assistance to the Controller

The Processor shall assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with the following obligations:

  • The obligation to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures (Article 32 GDPR).
  • The obligation to notify personal data breaches to the supervisory authority (Article 33 GDPR).
  • The obligation to communicate a personal data breach to the Data Subject (Article 34 GDPR).
  • The obligation to conduct data protection impact assessments (Article 35 GDPR).
  • The obligation to consult the supervisory authority prior to processing (Article 36 GDPR).

8. Technical & Organisational Measures

The Processor has implemented the following technical and organisational measures to protect Personal Data:

Technical measures

  • Encryption of personal data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3).
  • End-to-end encryption for synced habit content.
  • Hardware-backed key storage via device secure enclaves.
  • Certificate pinning for API communications.
  • Regular security audits and dependency vulnerability scanning.
  • Automated access controls with principle of least privilege.

Organisational measures

  • Data processing policies and procedures aligned with GDPR requirements.
  • Confidentiality agreements with all personnel handling Personal Data.
  • Incident response plan for data breaches.
  • Regular staff training on data protection and privacy.
  • Designated data protection contact (privacy@habitai.in).

9. Data Breach Notification

The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay (and in any event within 48 hours) after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting the Controller's data.

The notification shall include, to the extent available:

  • A description of the nature of the breach.
  • The categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned.
  • The likely consequences of the breach.
  • The measures taken or proposed to mitigate the breach.

The Processor shall cooperate fully with the Controller and provide all reasonable assistance in investigating and remediating the breach.

10. Audit & Compliance

The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. Upon the Controller's written request (at most once per 12-month period), the Processor shall provide a summary of its most recent security audit or SOC 2 report, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.

If the Controller requires an on-site audit, the parties shall agree on the scope, timing, and duration in advance. Any such audit shall be conducted during normal business hours and shall not unreasonably interfere with the Processor's operations.

11. Data Deletion & Return

Upon termination of the agreement or upon the Controller's request, the Processor shall, at the Controller's choice, delete or return all Personal Data processed on behalf of the Controller.

The Processor shall delete existing copies of Personal Data within 30 days, unless applicable law requires storage of the data. The Controller may also delete their data directly from within the application at any time.

12. Governing Law & Jurisdiction

This DPA shall be governed by the laws of the Republic of Ireland, which implements the GDPR. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this DPA shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Ireland.

This DPA does not restrict the rights of Data Subjects under the GDPR or any applicable data protection law.
