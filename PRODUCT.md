# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Health and wellness seekers who want to build, track, and break habits with minimal friction. They value voice input for quick check-ins, care about streaks and accountability, and want a tool that respects their attention without noise or upsells. Context: daily use, often on mobile, moments between activities (morning routine, commute, before bed).

## Product Purpose

HabitAI is the calmest habit tracker. It lets users speak habits into existence, track progress hands-free, and get private AI coaching without accounts, tracking, or ads. Success means users actually keep the app open and build lasting habits, not that they engage with the app more.

## Brand Personality

Modern, minimal, intelligent. Quiet confidence: the design gets out of the way so the habit becomes the focus. Voice and tone should feel like a trusted, calm assistant, not a motivational coach.

## Anti-references

- Typical fitness apps: no bright gradients, poppy CTAs, "hustle culture" energy, or aggressive gamification
- Generic AI/SaaS landing pages: no SaaS cream backgrounds, floating blobs, "AI-powered" buzzwords, or the warm-neutral AI monoculture
- No emoji-heavy copy, no exclamation marks, no "supercharge your productivity" language

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over noise** — Every element earns its place. Remove before adding.
2. **Show, don't tell** — Voice and interaction demos over bullet-point feature lists.
3. **Private by default** — Design communicates trust, local-first, zero tracking.
4. **Accessible to all** — WCAG AA, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, reduced motion respected.
5. **Practice what you preach** — The landing page should feel as calm and frictionless as the app itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Keyboard navigation throughout. Screen reader support with proper ARIA labels. Reduced motion via `prefers-reduced-motion`. High contrast ratios (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text). Touch targets minimum 44px.
